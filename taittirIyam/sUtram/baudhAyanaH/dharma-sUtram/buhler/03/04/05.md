5. When the day dawns, he drags himself away from the hinder part (of the skin), goes to a bathing-place, bathes (there) in the manner which is known, (but) performs, while in the water, sixteen suppressions of breath with the Aghamarshaá¹‡a hymn; next he performs the known (ceremonies) up to the worship of the sun, and afterwards goes to the house of his teacher. [^2] 


[^2]:  Govinda says that this manner of crawling out of the skin is symbolical of a new birth.
